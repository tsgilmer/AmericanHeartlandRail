# American Heartland Rail: Network-Based Intercity Rail Optimization

## Overview

This project develops and evaluates a proposed intercity passenger rail network connecting the Midwest and Southern United States using a simulation-based modeling framework.

---

## Key Insight

> Network topology and corridor chaining are more important than endpoint demand in determining rail system viability.

---

## Results

* ~9 million annual riders
* Farebox recovery > 1.3
* Benefit-Cost Ratio (BCR) > 2.4
* > 90% of simulations require little to no subsidy

---

## Methodology

* Graph-based network model (NetworkX)
* Gravity-based demand model
* Generalized cost modeling
* Monte Carlo simulation
